</p><h2>Dates Of Last Observation Of Unusual Minor Planets</h2>
The following tables list the date of the last astrometric observation reported
to the <a href="https://cgi.minorplanetcenter.net/iau/mpc.html">Minor Planet Center (MPC)</a> for each of the
unusual minor planets for which it is reasonable to make predictions (short-arc
unperturbed orbits from past years are excluded).  It is hoped that these
tables will assist observers in planning a useful program of astrometric
observation of these objects.  Ephemerides (from updated orbits for the
unnumbered objects listed, that are complete through the observations listed
below) may be generated using the <a href="https://cgi.minorplanetcenter.net/iau/MPEph/MPEph.html">on-line
Minor Planet Ephemeris Service</a>.
<p>
Observers are reminded of the efficiency of making and reporting observations
of specific objects on pairs of nearby nights, as the accuracy of an isolated
single-night observation can be difficult to judge.
</p><p>
In the lists below, the objects are divided into three categories:
one-opposition unnumbered objects; multiple-opposition unnumbered objects; and
numbered objects.  For reference, the following quantities are given for
each object: the uncertainty parameter U (where available), the arc in days
or the number of oppositions utilised in the orbit solution, and
low-precision values of the semi-major axis, eccentricity and inclination.
Objects observed within the past 15 days are indicated with an asterisk and
those objects unobserved within this period that are on the most recent
<a href="https://cgi.minorplanetcenter.net/iau/services/MPEC.html"><i>MPEC</i></a>
list of unusual objects are indicated with a `+'.
</p><p>
<b>Enhanced 2000 Dec. 26</b>.  The lists below now allow easy access to
MPES ephemerides of objects listed herein.  Use the checkboxes on the left-hand
side to select the objects (from any of the three lists) that you want to
observe.  <a href="https://cgi.minorplanetcenter.net/iau/lists/LastUnusual.html#opt">Select your observing options from the bottom of
the page</a> and then select `Get ephemerides'.  In the lists, the
data given under `Currently' is as follows: current R.A. in decimal hours;
current Decl. in decimal degrees; current V magnitude; current elongation;
and current motion in deg/day.  `Current' refers to the time this page was
last updated.
</p><p>
