2002 AA29 is in a 1:1 resonant orbit with Earth and is said to be "co-orbital". Press here to see the so-called Horeseshoe behaviour.

The simulation starts with Earth and the asteroid quite close and orbiting together. Use Tilt to see that the asteroid is in a slightly different plane to the Earth and crosses Earth's orbit so that they come very close. Speed up the integration and the distance between the asteroid and Earth gets slowly greater with the asteroid leading the Earth.

In the "Horeseshoe" view, rotating coordinates are used here to hold Earth in a constant direction relative to the Sun. AA29 can be seen making its way round to the other side of Earth. It will then slow down and allow Earth to move ahead. This behaviour is repeated and is called a horseshoe orbit.

The simulation includes Solar System planets barycentres and 2002AA29. Yoshida 6th order with variable step-times.
